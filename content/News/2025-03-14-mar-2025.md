Happy Pi Day! Here's a quick summary of some recent things going on with me:

- I got my [PhD](https://credentials.mit.edu/certificate/fb5dea8b0db8537293e2967b64e97777)!
  (I defended my [thesis](https://web.evanchen.cc/textbooks/chen-evanchen-phd-math-2025-thesis.pdf) last December.)

- My [multivariable calculus (18.02) notes are published on MIT OpenCourse
  Ware](https://ocw.mit.edu/courses/res-18-016-multivariable-calculus-recitation-notes-fall-2024/).

- The [2025 Teammate Hunt](https://2025.teammatehunt.com/)
  runs from March 28 to April 6. Please check it out!
  (Yes, I'm on the organizing team.)

- USEMO 2025 is open to all middle and high school students now
  (the US requirement has finally been dropped).
  Problem proposals are open now and are due on **May 10, 2025**;
  see the [USEMO page for submission instructions](https://web.evanchen.cc/usemo.html#volunteer).

- After being in development hell for 10 years,
  the silly weekend project I put together in 2015 is finally up:
  [Olympiad GeoGuessr](https://web.evanchen.cc/guessr/index.html),
  a dumb game where you can try to guess collinear and concyclic points
  from real MO diagrams.
  Thanks to Abdullahil Kafi for contributing a lot of the recent levels.

  We're going to call this a beta version because it hasn't been tested much.
  If you play enough, you're likely to spot some mistakes in the answer key.
  Please [report these](https://github.com/vEnhance/oly-geoguessr/issues?q=is%3Aissue%20state%3Aclosed)
  when you see them.

- The OTIS Mock AIME is now a data set on
  [Hugging Face](https://huggingface.co/datasets/EpochAI/otis-mock-aime-24-25)
  for AI enthusiasts. Shrug.

More generally, you can read about my general life status at my
[now page](https://web.evanchen.cc/now.html).
